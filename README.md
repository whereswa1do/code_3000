# code_3000
Collection of Assignments for CSE 3000: Contemporary Issues in Computer Science and Engineering

Ethical AI usage is such a hard topic to 'solve', it can't just be boiled down to a checklist of do's and don'ts. Simply setting rules doesn't work since context is so crucial. It gets more complicated when trying to figure out who or how guidelines for AI would be set. Governments are inherently unethical in my opinion (largely because of the individuals in power, not the government structure itself) and the public can't determine the guidelines since so many people are fueled by their own interests (think people jailbreaking AI). AI is way too powerful for one group to oversee ethics, but the best thing I could imagine is a panel of different actors: government officials, AI scientists, and regular people. Though unfeasible in terms of implementing, especially on a nationwide scale, I think the different viewpoints would cause checks and balances to come into play.



The most worrying thing to me is the pace in which AI is developing. Similar to cigarettes, AI is seen as the hot cool new thing that everyone wants to get their hands on. Problem is, many don't care to think about the negative effects of AI, both short and long term. What doesn't help is AI is actually useful in a way smoking a cigarette to 'take the edge off' never could be.
